Cold & Flu

 Cough

  Ingridents:

  • Adhatoda zeylanica leaf juice
  • Honey
  • Allium cepa
  • Piper longum
  • Terminalia bellirica
  • Zingiber officinale

  Procedure:

  1. A mixture of adhatoda zeylanica leaf juice (6ml) and honey (4ml) is given in cough and sore throat.
  2. Allium cepa bulb juice with honey is very effective in cough and colds. for this, freshly prepared, 1 teaspoonful juice of raw allium cepa mixed with 11/2 teaspoonful of honey should be taken. can be given to children suffering from cough.
  3. In whooping cough, equal amount of piper longum fruit powder, zingiber officinale powder and Terminalia bellirica powder is mixed and taken along with honey.

 Sinusitis

  Ingridents:

  • Vasanthakusumakaram
  • thumbai
  • Chukku or dried ginger
  • Thippli, Milagu, Omam, Manjal
  • Vediuppu

  Procedure:

  1. Vasanthakusumakaram mathirai with thumbai and thulasi charu
  2. Thrikadagu choornam, palakarai parpam, muthuchipi parpam etc
  3. To 100 parts of chukku or dried ginger, one part of camphor is mixed, this mixture is placed over a piece of cloth. This cloth is rolled as stick, and it is burnt. The smoke coming from this wick is allowed to be inhaled by the patient.
  4. Similarly thippli, milagu, omam, manjal are made into paste, applied in a cloth, wicks prepared and smoke inhale. This is very effective for pus and watery discharge from nose.
  5. Equal amount of vediuppu, manjal,milagu is made to paste and used for fumigation.

 Common Cold

  Ingridents:

  • Tulsi
  • Dried ginger
  • Honey
  • Black pepper
  • Amla

  Procedure:

  1. Leaves of holy basil (Tulsi) plant mixed with an equal quantity of dried ginger powder form an excellent substitute for tea during cold and flu. This may be taken by adding milk and sugar three times a day.
  2. Juice of ginger (Adrak) and holy basil (Tulsi) one-one tea spoon (5 ml.) mixed with one tea spoon (5 ml.) honey, to be taken two-three times a day.
  3. Powder of black pepper (marich) half tea spoon (2.5 ml.) mixed with jaggery (guda) two times a day.
  4. Two to three tea spoon (10-15 ml.) of fresh juice of Amla or half to one tea spoon (2.5-5 gm) powder of the same is ideal to control cold.

 Fever

  Ingridents:

  • Black Pepper
  • Ginger
  • Holy Basil
  • Rose water
  • Garlic

  Procedure:

  1. Sponging the forehead and the body with cold water is a commonly recommended practice in high fever and in fever due to sun-stroke. For better results, add 1 teaspoonful of rose water to the cold water before sponging. You could even add the rose water to an ice-bag and use it for sponging.
  2. Take about 20 fresh leaves of Holy Basil. Take a small piece of ginger and about 5 grains of black pepper. Boil them together in your tea and drink the warm liquid.
  3. Add 2-3 crushed pods of garlic to a quarter cup of warm water drink it or have it in the form of soup which serves as comforting food and reduces the fever.
